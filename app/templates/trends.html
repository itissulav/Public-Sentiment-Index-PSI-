1. Overall Sentiment Split

i
What this shows: This doughnut chart divides all analyzed posts into Positive, Negative, and Neutral categories.

Meaning: Gives you an immediate macro-view of the general public mood. A massive negative slice indicates widespread discontent.

2. Sentiment Timeline (Daily Volume)

i
What this shows: Traces the number of posts for each sentiment category day by day.

Meaning: Helps identify specific spikes in emotion. For example, a sudden peak in negative posts likely correlates to a real-world controversial event on that date.

3. Average Upvotes by Emotion

i
What this shows: The average upvote score received by posts, grouped by their sentiment.

Meaning: Reveals what type of content the community rewards. If negative posts have the highest average, the platform heavily incentivizes outrage or critical discussion.

4. Positivity vs. Engagement

i
What this shows: Scatter plot comparing a post's positive AI probability score (X-axis) against its upvote score (Y-axis).

Meaning: Identifies if highly positive posts systematically go viral, or if high engagement is clustered around lower positivity (controversy).

5. Top 10 Most Viral Posts Profile

i
What this shows: The upvote scores and assigned sentiment of the 10 absolute most popular posts in the dataset.

Meaning: Highlights the extreme outliers. The tone of these top 10 posts often sets the culture for the entire community.

6. Sentiment Volatility Index

i
What this shows: The standard deviation (variance) of positive sentiment day-by-day.

Meaning: Peaks indicate days of extreme polarization where users were heavily split between intense joy and intense anger. Flat lines indicate uniform consensus.

7. Negative Intensity Distribution

i
What this shows: Groups negative scores into severity brackets (0.0 to 1.0 probability).

Meaning: Determines if negative posts are just mildly critical (lower scores) or intensely furious/toxic (scores closer to 1.0).

8. Positive Intensity Distribution

i
What this shows: Groups positive scores into severity brackets.

Meaning: Shows if optimistic posts are just mildly agreeable or fiercely enthusiastic and supportive.

9. Informational (Neutral) Volume

i
What this shows: The outright volume of purely neutral/objective posts over time.

Meaning: Tracks the flow of unbiased news and factual reporting during the election period, separating it from opinionated rhetoric.

10. Post Volume by Hour

i
What this shows: Total posts aggregated by the time of day they were published (00:00 - 23:00).

Meaning: Reveals the peak active hours for the public to engage in political discussion.

11. Systemic AI Probability Baseline

i
What this shows: A radar chart of the average underlying probability scores (Negative, Neutral, Positive) across the ENTIRE dataset.

Meaning: Shows the fundamental baseline bias of the data. Even if a post is classified "Neutral", it might have structural underlying negativity.

12. Text Length vs Sentiment

i
What this shows: Character length of posts (X-axis) plotted against their positivity probability score (Y-axis).

Meaning: Answers the question: "Are longer, detailed rants more likely to be deeply negative or highly positive?"

13. The Viral Curve (Score Distribution)

i
What this shows: How many posts fall into specific upvote brackets (e.g., 0-10 upvotes, 1k+ upvotes).

Meaning: Illustrates the platform's inequality. Typically shows that 95% of posts get ignored while 5% go massively viral.

14. Cumulative Discussion Growth

i
What this shows: A running total of all posts made over the tracked timeframe.

Meaning: The steepness of the curve indicates momentum. A sudden vertical climb means an explosion in public interest.

15. Sentiment by Day of the Week

i
What this shows: Total post volume broken down by Monday through Sunday, split by sentiment color.

Meaning: Discovers weekly behavioral trends. For instance, are users significantly more negative and combative on Mondays compared to weekends?
